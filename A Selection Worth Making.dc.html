01 — The paradox: leads on brand, loses at shelf

MCS leads or ties Hell on brand power metrics — yet loses the shelf decision. It’s in the consideration set: consumers know it, value it, but the impulse moment tips toward Hell. RTD coffee is a strongly impulse-driven category: the final decision happens in the three seconds spent in front of the cooler.

02 — The untapped asset: deliberate choice

The MCS range — classic and flavoured, lactose-free, no added sugar — has never been positioned as a strategic statement. The breadth was always the real strength, but never became something a consumer could feel proud of choosing. Nine years of SKU development is what gives the word ‘Selection’ meaning — not a naming choice, but a portfolio reality.

‘Deliberate choice’ is not a coffee-connoisseur attitude — it’s the most everyday expression of self-respect: the consumer knows what they want. The gifting dimension follows from this: the same logic that drives picking one for yourself drives picking one for someone else.

02 — Big Idea
The Pause Before You Pick.
↓
Campaign Essence

Built around moments of deliberate choice. Small, low-stakes, quietly meaningful. Not dramatic decisions — the everyday ones. Which blend to open this morning. Which pack to slip into a colleague's bag. Which coffee to leave on a friend's desk after a hard week.

Creative Concept

Every execution captures the moment just before a selection is made. A hand hovering over options. A slight consideration. The decision landed on. Then the outcome — not a grand scene, just a quiet satisfaction. Or a colleague's face when they find something waiting.

The Gifting Dimension — Small gesture. Deliberate choice.

The most personal gift isn't expensive — it's selected. When you give someone Mizo Coffee Selection, the message is: I thought about what you'd like, and I picked this. No occasion required. No bow. The choosing is the point.

The gifting mechanic captures the specific warmth of spontaneous consideration. Not a birthday gift. The extra scarf someone brought because it got cold. The coffee left on your desk because a colleague was already at the machine and thought of you.

05 — Media Strategy
2 phases · deliberate, not loud.
↓
Phase 1 — Relaunch + Positioning (Weeks 1–4)

TVC and pre-roll (15" and 30") establish the 'pause before you pick' visual language with widest reach. OOH in high-footfall retail — supermarket entrances, transport hubs. The media approach mirrors the brand: deliberate, not loud. The brand re-enters the market calmly.

Phase 2 — Gifting Activation (Weeks 3–8, overlapping)

In-store display at the point of decision — coffee aisle and checkout impulse zone. Meta + TikTok targeting reaches gifting moments: back-to-office season, autumn shift. Micro-influencer layer: real people, small followings, actual gifts. Not haul content — one coffee, one desk, one genuine moment.

Channel Priorities
TV / VODBrand anchor, broad reach — establishes the visual language
In-storeThe actual moment of selection — critical for a supermarket pick-up product
Social — Meta / TikTokGifting mechanic, earned and organic amplification
OOHNear-retail tactical. Warm, considered creative.
What to avoid
Fragrance-style aspirational placement. Heavy influencer gift PR defeats the intimacy. The media strategy should feel as considered as the brand promise.

07 — Campaign Across Touchpoints
In-store · Gifting SKU · Packaging · Digital · Print
↓
In-Store — End-of-aisle display

Instead of a standard product stack, the display presents varieties side by side with small behavioural descriptor cards — not tasting notes, but occasion cues. 'For the morning you own.' 'For the afternoon you share.' The gifting SKU sits at the display and again in the checkout impulse zone.

Gifting SKU — "For You."

Gift-ready sleeve of 4 capsules, curated varieties, designed to be grabbed at checkout alongside your own pack. Minimal copy needed on the outer sleeve — the Mizo Coffee Selection branding provides context, the lead communication is the act of giving.

Packaging Integration

Campaign tagline on secondary packaging. The inner panel — the moment you open the box and choose a capsule — carries a single line: 'What will it be?' Activates the selection metaphor at the exact right moment.

Digital / Social

Statics and short-form video anchored in the selection moment: hands, options, the pause. For gifting: real situations, minimal staging. A coffee left at reception. A pack tucked into a gym bag. A message that just says 'left you something.' UGC: #MySelection.

Print / Press

Single editorial-style image, no product arrangement, no recipe. The selection moment, shot cleanly. The tagline. The pack. Nothing else.

08 — Consumer Engagement + Viral
5 mechanics — low friction, high resonance.
↓
1. #MySelection

Dead simple. Share what you picked, or who you picked it for. The gifting angle gives it genuine emotional fuel: 'Left this for my colleague on her last day. She always took the espresso.' That's a real post. The mechanic needs no incentive — the gesture is the content.

2. The Desk Drop

A gifting ritual seeded by the brand. Leave a capsule on someone's desk, no note. Photograph it, post it, tag someone. Seed with micro-creators in real workplaces, then let it run without brand overlay.

3. "You Know What They'd Pick" Generator

A simple online tool or Stories quiz: answer 5 questions about someone and it recommends which Mizo variety they'd choose. Output shareable: 'I think [Name] is a Dark Roast morning person.' Tags the person, invites them to confirm or dispute.

4. The Selection Filter

Instagram/TikTok filter that 'selects' a Mizo variety based on mood, time of day, or who you're with. The mechanic mirrors the campaign's central idea: even when a tool picks for you, someone made the decision to use it.

5. In-Store Selection Challenge

POS mechanic: a small card asks you to pick a variety based on a behavioural descriptor, not the product name. Scan to reveal which variety you chose + chance to win a full Selection box. Drives trial of non-default varieties.
